Bio

My passion for Software Development did not start as early as that. Since I was a little boy, I was always curious about how certain things work and, at the time, it intrigued me a lot about how a box with electronic components inside, could connect a whole world, as well as produce absolutely incredible things. With this in mind, at 18 years, I enrolled in the Bachelors in Information Systems and Computer Engineering at the Instituto Superior Técnico. Without any prior experience in programming, at first everything seemed to me a little abstract, but my curiosity and motivation to know more and to be better, made me face all the obstacles I had to overcome.

After much work and dedication, I finished my Bachelors Degree. Then, I decided to join the Master in Information Systems and Computer Engineering at the same institution. Why? I found out that I needed some more formation, because I did not felt prepared for the job market. Given the several specializations that we could choose, I opted for Software Engineering and Distributed Systems.

And it was at the end of this Masters, that I began a great project, in which I still work today. "dDocs - A Citizen-Centric Electronic Document and Record Management System". This was the theme of my thesis, an idea coming from a startup - MDSS, Meta Documents Systems, LDA - which consisted in designing and implementing a platform in which we could have our documents as citizens (Citizen's Card, Driver's License, etc.) in digital format, with all the security and privacy issues that underlie this problem, as well as its integration with public entities of a State so that the documents could be completed in real time and only at the user's request: the citizen's information is never stored on the platform.

Having said that, in addition to the dDocs design, I implemented a small prototype to serve as proof of concept with well-known technologies: PHP for back-end, HTML / Javascript / JQuery / CSS for front-end and MySQL as database.

Even before completing my Master's degree, I was integrated into the dDocs development team at MDSS in order to develop the actual product, using .NET Core, Angular (2/4/5) and PostgreSQL.

Later, I was also involved in the development of another project: PoL - Proof of Life. This project is based on safeguarding passwords of citizens, should any fatality occur to them, sent to people decided by the user in his life span.

In early 2018, I decided I wanted to experience something new professionally so I decided to try some freelancing work! And that's where I am right now!
